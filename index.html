Robotics Showcase

ProjectJACOB is a search and rescue robot designed to identify victims in disaster scenarios. Using ComputerVision and Microsoft's Azure machine learning software, this bot can learn tricks faster than your chihuahua. The magic lies within the balance of advanced code and battle-tested robotics hardware. This project takes advantage of Adept Technology Inc.'s powerful P3-AT rover and XBOX360 Kinect. The rover ploughs through rugged terrain while the Kinect searches for human faces.

We customized our OpenCV code to optimize the Kinect's ability to detect and communicate with Azure when it sees a human face. The machine learning process allows this cycle to become more accurate and reliable through increased use. This technology has the potential to identify victims that have been trapped in disaster areas too hostile for humans to explore.

Details :


  • The P3-AT rover uses 3 lead-acid batteries, totalling to a hefty 252 Watt-hours of capacity. This allows for over 6 hours of battery life during deployment.
  • Using Microsoft Azure, we developed a machine learning model that acts as a neural network. This allows the machine to constantly improve its facial recognition capabilities with OpenCV computer vision technology.
  • The Canadian government spends $8.1M on developments in Search and Rescue each year. This field is an important part of the country's capabilities, and this budget has been fixed for over 20 years.
  • Pioneer robots have been used for classroom and research purposes for the past 15 years. Their versatility has proven them essentail in testing a variety of models.

Inspiration :

In March 2016, our community was struck with the loss of a young student. The days following the tragedy were marked by displays of grief, confusion, and most importantly-- solidarity. The loss of a single life devastated an entire community, but we stood strong. This very strength inspired our developer team to dedicate this RoboHack in honor of his memory. We are proud to present ProjectJACOB.

ProjectJACOB is an elegant blend of rugged hardware and intelligent software. The aim of this robot is to recognize victims trapped in disaster zones based on facial recognition technology. This machine is capable of great learning feats, improving its facial recognition ability with every encounter it has. We believe that advancements in search and rescue technology will allow affected communities to recover from uncontrollable disasters.
